M2HRI: Multimodal Multi-Agent Human-Robot Interaction

An LLM-Driven Framework for Personalized Multi-Robot Interaction

Abstract

Multi-robot systems are increasingly deployed in social environments, yet most existing approaches treat robots as identical agents. In this work, we introduce M2HRI, a multimodal multi-agent framework that enables distinct robot personalities, long-term memory for personalization, multimodal perception (vision and speech), and centralized coordination for coherent interaction. Our approach models agent individuality alongside coordinated behavior, allowing robots to adapt to users and maintain consistent interactions over time. Experimental results demonstrate that incorporating personality, memory, and multimodal perception significantly improves interaction quality, engagement, and personalization in multi-robot systems.
